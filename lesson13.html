1. Why Goal Setting Matters

Everything you've learned in this course — about your body, relationships, STIs, decision-making, and resisting pressure — comes together in this final lesson. The question is: What are you going to do with this knowledge?

Key insight: Knowing your plans and goals for the future helps you make safe and healthy decisions now. When you have a clear picture of where you want to go in life, it's easier to avoid things that could derail you.

An unplanned pregnancy or an HIV/STI infection will have an impact on your overall plans and goals. This isn't meant to scare you — it's meant to empower you to protect your future.

✚ A Note for Our Family:

God has plans for your life — good plans, plans to give you a hope and a future. Setting goals isn't just practical wisdom — it's partnering with God in shaping the life He has for you.

"'For I know the plans I have for you,' declares the Lord, 'plans to prosper you and not to harm you, plans to give you hope and a future.'" — Jeremiah 29:11

2. The Goal-Setting Process

6 Steps to Setting and Achieving a Goal

  1. Write your goal — Be specific. What exactly do you want to achieve?
  2. List the steps — What do you need to do, in order, to reach this goal?
  3. Identify obstacles — What life events or personal habits could make it difficult?
  4. Identify helpers — Who can help you? What resources are available?
  5. Take the first step — What is the very first thing you can do right now?
  6. Get started, take responsibility, and regularly assess your progress
SMART Goals: Make your goals Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. "I want to do well in school" is vague. "I want to maintain a 3.5 GPA this semester by studying at least 1 hour every weekday" is a SMART goal.

3. Sample Goal-Setting Worksheet

Step 1: Write your goal

"I want to graduate from high school with a strong GPA and get into a good college where I can study computer science."

Step 2: Things I must do to achieve this goal

"1. Study every day and complete all homework on time
2. Take challenging classes (honors/AP when possible)
3. Stay active in extracurricular activities
4. Avoid risky behaviors that could derail my plans (drugs, unprotected sex, etc.)"

Step 3: Obstacles that could make it difficult

"1. Peer pressure to skip studying or engage in risky behavior
2. Procrastination and distraction (social media, video games)
3. An unplanned pregnancy or STI could completely change my path"

Step 4: People or resources that could help

"1. My parents — for guidance, support, and accountability
2. My teachers and school counselor — for academic help and advice
3. My church community — for spiritual support and mentorship"

Step 5: First things I need to do NOW

"1. Set up a daily study schedule and stick to it
2. Talk to my parents about my goals
3. Sign my personal contract committing to protect my health and future"

4. My Personal Contract to Protect My Sexual Health

The course ends with a personal contract — a commitment you make to yourself about protecting your health and your future.

My Personal Contract

Step 1: Name & Age

Write your name and age.

Step 2: Reasons I want to protect myself from HIV/STIs and an unplanned pregnancy

"I want to protect myself because I have big goals for my future. I want to go to college, build a career, and eventually have a family when I'm ready. An STI or unplanned pregnancy would make those goals much harder to achieve. I also want to honor God's plan for my body and my life. I value my health, my appearance, my family, and my future career — all of which could be affected by risky decisions."

Step 3: I pledge to protect my sexual health by:

"1. Choosing abstinence — waiting until marriage for sexual intimacy
2. Avoiding drugs and alcohol, which impair my judgment
3. Using assertive communication to resist peer pressure
4. Staying away from risky situations and having an escape plan
5. Being a critical consumer of media messages"

Step 4: One person I will talk to about protecting my sexual health:

"My parents (Mom and Dad)"

Step 5: One person who will support me:

"My parents, and my church small group leader"
✚ A Note for Our Family:

This contract is more than a school assignment — it's a declaration of who you want to be and how you want to live. As a family, we commit to supporting you in these goals, praying for you, and being someone you can always talk to. You are never alone in this.

"Commit to the Lord whatever you do, and he will establish your plans." — Proverbs 16:3

5. Community Resources

Remember: there are clinics and resources in your community where you can get confidential help:

  • HIV/STI testing — available at clinics, health departments, and some pharmacies
  • Pregnancy testing — available at clinics and doctor's offices
  • Many services are confidential, low-cost, or free for teens
  • You can also talk to your family doctor about any health concerns
Protecting your sexual health is a life-long responsibility. The knowledge you've gained in this course gives you the power to make informed, responsible decisions throughout your life.

6. Key Terms

Goal Setting: The process of identifying what you want to achieve and creating a plan to get there.
SMART Goals: Goals that are Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound.
Personal Contract: A commitment you make to yourself (and share with a trusted adult) about how you will protect your health.
Accountability: Being responsible for your choices and having someone who helps you stay on track.
Life-long Responsibility: Protecting your sexual health is not a one-time decision but an ongoing commitment throughout your life.

7. Worksheet Answers (Lesson Wrap-Up)

Lesson 13 (Goal-Setting) — True or False

Statement Answer Why?
Knowing your plans and goals for the future may help you make safe and healthy decisions now. TRUE When you know where you want to go in life, it's easier to make decisions that protect your future. Goals give you motivation to avoid risky behavior.
Experiencing an unplanned pregnancy or becoming HIV infected will not have an impact on your overall plans and goals. FALSE An unplanned pregnancy or HIV infection would significantly impact your plans — education, career, finances, relationships, and health would all be affected.
Protecting the sexual health of yourself and others is a life-long responsibility. TRUE Sexual health isn't just a teen issue. It's a life-long responsibility that continues into adulthood and marriage. The habits and knowledge you build now serve you for your entire life.

Lesson 13 (HIV/STI Testing & Community Resources) — True or False

Statement Answer Why?
There are clinics in the community where you can get confidential, low-cost, or free HIV/STI and pregnancy tests. TRUE Many communities offer confidential, low-cost, or free testing services at clinics, health departments, and community health centers.
Methods to avoid an HIV/STI infection or an unplanned pregnancy include abstaining from sexual activity or using latex condoms consistently and correctly. TRUE Abstinence is 100% effective. Using latex condoms consistently and correctly significantly reduces (but doesn't eliminate) the risk of STIs and pregnancy.
Limiting sexual contact to only one person may reduce but not eliminate the risk of HIV/STI infection. TRUE Mutual monogamy (both partners only with each other) reduces risk, but doesn't eliminate it — because either partner could have an existing infection they don't know about.
