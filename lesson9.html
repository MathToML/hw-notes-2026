1. What Are STIs?

STIs (Sexually Transmitted Infections) are infections passed from one person to another through sexual contact. Key facts:

  • STIs can be caused by bacteria, viruses, or parasites
  • The most common symptom of an STI is NOTHING — many STIs show no visible symptoms, which is why they spread so easily
  • STIs can be transmitted through vaginal, anal, or oral sex, and sometimes through skin-to-skin contact
  • Some STIs can be cured with antibiotics (bacterial ones); others can only be treated but not cured (viral ones)
  • Vaccines exist for Hepatitis B and HPV
Most important fact: Many people with STIs don't know they have one because there are often NO symptoms. This is why prevention and testing are so important.
✚ A Note for Our Family:

Learning about STIs isn't about expecting you to be sexually active. It's about being informed so you can make wise decisions and understand why God's design for sexual intimacy within marriage provides protection. Knowledge is a tool for wisdom.

74,702 new cases in 2015		 Direct contact with a syphilitic sore (chancre), mother to unborn child Stage 1: painless sore. Stage 2: rash, fever, swollen glands. 3. Viral STIs (NOT Curable)

STIHow It SpreadsSymptomsConsequencesTreatment
HPV (Human Papillomavirus)
20M infected in US		 Skin-to-skin contact, sexual contact, even if no visible warts Most people (9/10) have no symptoms. May cause genital warts Can cause cervical, vulvar, penile, anal, and throat cancers NOT curable. Warts can be removed. Vaccine available (recommended ages 11-12)
Herpes (HSV-1, HSV-2)
45-60M in US infected with HSV-2		 Direct contact with infected skin, sores, or mucous membranes. Can spread even without visible sores Painful blisters or sores on genitals, rectum, or mouth. Women may have sores on cervix (painless) Lifelong outbreaks, increased HIV risk. Herpes encephalitis is rare but can be fatal to newborns NOT curable. Medication can reduce outbreaks and transmission
Hepatitis B (HBV) Blood, semen, vaginal fluids, shared needles, mother to infant Fever, nausea, jaundice (yellow skin), dark urine. Some have no symptoms About 6% become infectious for life. Can cause liver damage/cancer, death NOT curable. Immune system usually clears it. Vaccine available
HIV Blood, semen, vaginal fluids, breast milk, shared needles, mother to infant May have no symptoms for years Destroys immune system, leads to AIDS, opportunistic infections, death NOT curable. ART medication can control it (covered in Lesson 10)
Zika Mosquito bites, sexual contact, mother to fetus Mild: fever, rash, red eyes, joint pain. Often no symptoms Pregnancy: birth defects (microcephaly), miscarriage, stillbirth No cure. Diagnosis by travel history and blood test

5. Prevention & Testing

  • Abstinence is the only 100% effective way to prevent STIs
  • Condoms reduce the risk of many STIs but do not eliminate it (especially for skin-to-skin STIs like herpes and HPV)
  • Vaccines are available for Hepatitis B and HPV — get vaccinated!
  • Regular testing is important for anyone who is sexually active, since many STIs have no symptoms
  • Mutual monogamy (being with only one uninfected partner) reduces risk
  • Don't share needles or drug equipment
Key takeaway: All the incurable STIs (HIV, HPV, Herpes, Hepatitis B, Zika) are caused by viruses. Bacterial STIs (Chlamydia, Gonorrhea, Syphilis) can be cured with antibiotics if caught early. Prevention is always better than treatment.

7. Lesson Wrap-Up Answers

Part I — True or False

Statement Answer Why?
Hepatitis B and HPV can be prevented with a vaccine. TRUE Vaccines exist for both Hepatitis B and HPV. The HPV vaccine is recommended for everyone starting at ages 11-12.
The only way an STI can be spread from one person to another is through penis-vagina contact. FALSE STIs can spread through many types of contact: vaginal sex, anal sex, oral sex, skin-to-skin contact (herpes, HPV), shared needles, blood, and from mother to baby during birth.
The most common symptom of an STI is nothing. TRUE Many STIs have no visible symptoms, which is why they spread so easily and why regular testing is important for sexually active people.

Part II — What was the most important thing you learned?

"I learned that the most common symptom of an STI is actually nothing — many people don't even know they're infected. I also learned the difference between bacterial STIs (which can be cured with antibiotics) and viral STIs (which cannot be cured and stay with you for life). This really shows why abstinence is the safest choice."

Part III — How committed are you to protecting yourself and others from an STI?

Circle 1 (Not Committed) to 5 (Very Committed). A strong answer is 5 (Very Committed).
